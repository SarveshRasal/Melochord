Ensemble Performance

Group Composition: Ensembles should consist of two or more performers. The composition can vary from duets to larger groups like quartets, quintets, or full bands.
Instrumentation: Ensembles can feature a mix of instruments such as strings, woodwinds, brass, percussion, and vocals. The choice of instruments is open to participants' preferences and musical genres.
Repertoire: Ensembles should prepare and perform a piece of music of their choice. The chosen repertoire should highlight the ensemble's skills and showcase their collective musicality.
Performance Duration: Each ensemble will be allotted a specific performance time. Performances should adhere to the allotted time to ensure fairness and smooth event flow.
Originality: While original compositions are welcome, participants can also perform arrangements or adaptations of existing pieces. Proper credit and permissions must be obtained for any copyrighted material.
Adjudication: Performances will be judged by a panel of experienced musicians or music educators. Judging criteria may include technical skill, interpretation, ensemble cohesion, communication, and overall musical impact.
Props and Staging: Ensembles are allowed to use basic props or staging elements that enhance their performance, but these should not overshadow the musical presentation.
Dress Code: Participants should dress appropriately for their performance, considering the genre and formality of the event.
Equipment: Ensembles are responsible for bringing their own instruments and any required equipment. The event organizers will provide basic sound amplification if needed.
Respectful Conduct: All ensemble members should display professionalism and respect toward fellow participants, event staff, judges, and the audience.

Solo Performances

Performance Duration: Each solo performer will be allocated a specific performance time. Performances should adhere to the allotted time to ensure fairness and smooth event flow.
Repertoire: Solo performers should prepare and perform a piece of music of their choice. The chosen repertoire should highlight the performer's skills and showcase their individual musicality.
Originality: While original compositions are welcome, participants can also perform covers or arrangements of existing pieces. Proper credit and permissions must be obtained for any copyrighted material.
Instrumentation: Solo performances can feature a wide range of instruments, including voice. Participants should bring their own instruments unless alternative arrangements have been made.
Adjudication: Performances will be judged by a panel of experienced musicians or music educators. Judging criteria may include technical skill, interpretation, expression, communication, and overall musical impact.
Props and Staging: Solo performers are allowed to use basic props or staging elements that enhance their performance, but these should not overshadow the musical presentation.
Dress Code: Participants should dress appropriately for their performance, considering the genre and formality of the event.
Equipment: Solo performers are responsible for bringing their own instruments and any required equipment. The event organizers will provide basic sound amplification if needed.
Respectful Conduct: Solo performers should display professionalism and respect toward event staff, judges, and the audience.

Instrumental Showcase

Performance Duration: Each instrumentalist will be granted a precise performance time of 4 minutes. Performances should conclude within this time to ensure fairness and event scheduling.
Repertoire: Participants are required to perform a single instrumental piece of their choosing. The chosen piece must showcase the capabilities and nuances of their selected instrument.
Originality: While participants are encouraged to perform original compositions, covers or arrangements of existing pieces are also accepted. Ensure proper attribution for copyrighted material.
Instrument Preparation: All instrumentalists must ensure their instruments are tuned and properly maintained before their performance slot.
Adjudication: Performances will be assessed by a panel of skilled judges with expertise in various instruments. Criteria include technical proficiency, expressive interpretation, tone quality, originality, and stage presence.
Dress Code: Participants should dress in appropriate concert attire that aligns with the formality of the event.
Props and Staging: Limited use of props that enhance the musical presentation is allowed, but they should complement the performance rather than overshadow it.
Respectful Conduct: All participants must demonstrate professionalism and respect towards event staff, fellow performers, judges, and the audience.

Melody Mastermind

Team Formation: Teams can consist of 2 to 4 members. Teams will compete collaboratively to answer quiz questions.
Question Format: Questions will encompass diverse music-related categories, including music history, genres, famous composers, lyrics, and instrument recognition.
Answering Protocol: Teams will nominate a spokesperson to provide answers. Responses will be provided orally, and teams should await the quizmaster's acknowledgment before answering.
Time Limits: Each question will have a 30-second response time limit to maintain an efficient quiz pace.
Scoring: Correct answers earn teams points. Scores will be calculated cumulatively, and the team with the highest score at the end of the quiz wins.
Fair Play: Teams are expected to adhere to the rules and exhibit sportsmanship throughout the quiz.
Tie-Breakers: In case of tied scores, sudden-death tie-breaker questions will determine the winner.
Moderation: A designated quizmaster will read out questions, manage the quiz flow, and ensure fairness.
